CSOs

1. To what extent does the state provide favourable conditions for an effective role of CSOs?

58.33% of respondents find that the state moderately provides favorable conditions for them to have an effective role. Other respondents have leaned towards expressing low favorable conditions provided to them by the state.

2. Are you familiar with how legal provisions, regulations, or policies regarding the functioning of CSOs in Kosovo have changed? If yes, how would you assess those changes.

41.67% of respondents do not believe that they are familiar with changes on legal provisions, regulations, or policies regarding the functioning of CSOs in Kosovo. The remaining 58.33% believe that they are familiar with the changes among which 25% of them believe that the situation has worsened.

3. To what extent CSOs play strong and effective role in Kosovo?

More than half of the respondents (58.33%) believe that the role that CSOs play is moderate, while 33.33% believe that the role played by CSOS ranges from high to very high.

4. Is there a legal document that regulates the cooperation between authorities and CSOs?

Half of the polled CSOs (50%) claim that there exists a legal document that regulates the cooperation between authorities and CSOs whereas the other half either replied no or abstained (25%) from answering (25%).

5. To what extent are public authorities establishing a constructive cooperation with CSOs (the level of communication, participation in joint initiatives etc.)?

Respondants have been equally divided on answering this question: 1/3 find that cooperation between public authorities with CSOs is moderate, another 1/3 find it low and the remaining 1/3 find the cooperating to be high.

6. Is your country member of Open Government Partnership (OGP)?

More than the half of the polled CSOs (58.33%) said yes despite the fact that Kosovo is not a member of the Open Government Partnership (OGP).

6.1. If your country is a member of Open Government Partnership (OGP), is there a national task force that includes representatives of CSOs?

From the respondants who said that their country is a member of Open Government Partnership (OGP), which is not the case, 57.14% of them said are under the impression that there is a national task force that includes representatives of CSOs.

6.2. If your country is member of Open Government Partnership (OGP), to what extent CSOs have the influence on that process?

From the respondants who said that their country is a member of Open Government Partnership (OGP), which is not true, 50% of them are under the impression that influence of CSOs is at hight level.

7. To what extent CSOs are part of the policy-making process?

1/3 of the polled CSOs (33.33%) believe that the inclusion of CSOs on the policy-making process is high. Another 1/3 (33.33%) of respondents choose claime that the influence is moderate. The remainder of respondents (66.66%) are split between at high (33.33%) and low level (33.33%).

8. Which of the following mechanism for the cooperation with institutions do you consider as the most effective?

Half of the polled CSOs (50%) find that the most effective mechanisms for cooperation with institutions is organizing joint public discussions, round tables, seminars and other forms of joint activities. 33.33% prefer participation in working groups and other bodies formed by the state administration bodies. The remainder (16.67%) believe in personal contacts with stakeholders . No other mechanisms were proposed.

9. Which of the following mechanisms for obtaining information, which are not publicly disclosed, from the institutions do you consider as the most effective?

41.67% polled CSOs believe that FOI requests is the most effective mechanisms for obtaining information, which are not publicly disclosed, whereas 33.33% of the respondents prefer official channels of communications. The remaining 25% place their trust on personal contacts.

10. To what extent can CSOs influence the application of good governance standards by state institutions in your country (Transparency, Accountability, Accessibility, Participation, Interaction etc.)?

Half of the polled CSOs (50%) believe that CSOs have moderate influence on the application of good governance standards by state institutions. The other half is split between high (25%) and very high influence (25%).

11. When asking information from state institutions, how prompt are they in their answers?

  • 1/3 of the polled media (33.33%) find that judicial insitutions are moderately prompt in providing answers. Another 1/3 (33.33%) of respondents choose claime that the level is hight. The remainder of respondents (33.33%) are split between at low (16.16%) and very hight (16.16%).
  • 50% of respondents find that legislative insitutions are moderately prompt in providing answers, 25% find it hight and 8.3 very high.The remainder of respondents 16.66% choose low level.
  • Half of the polled CSOs (50%) believe that executive institutions are hightly prompt in providing answers. 33.33% find it moderately promt and 16.16% find it low.



Media

1. To what extent does the state provide favorable conditions for an effective role of media?

71.43% of respondents find that the state moderately provides favorable conditions for them to have an effective role. The remaining respondents perceive a low degree of favorable conditions provided by the state.

2. Are you familiar with how legal provisions, regulations or policies regarding the functioning of media in Kosovo have changed? If yes, how would you assess those changes?

85.71% of respondents claim they are familiar of which 71.43% believe that the situation has not changed significantly and 14.29% believe that the situation has improved. 14.29% of respondents claim they are not familiar.

3. To what extent are the public authorities establishing a constructive cooperation with media? (the level of communication, equal access to information etc.).

85.71% of the respondents believe that the cooperation of authorities with media is at moderate. Only 14.29% believe that there is a low level of cooperation.

4. To what extent do state institutions provide an equal treatment of all media?

  • 57.14% of the respondents find that judicial institutions provide moderate level of equality whereas the remaining 42.86% find that there is a low level of equal treatment.
  • 71.43% of the respondents find that legislative institutions provide moderate level of equality with the rest equally divided between high and very high.
  • 42.86% of the respondents find that executive institutions provide low level of equality, another 42.86% believe they provide moderate levels of equality and 14.29% believe they provide high level of equality.

5. To what extent can the media influence the application of good governance standards by state institutions in Kosovo? (Transparency, Accountability, Accessibility, Participation, Interaction etc.)

  • 71.43% of respondents find that media has moderate influence on judicial institutions whereas the remaining 28.57% find that there is low level influence.
  • 85.71% of respondents find that media has moderate influence on legislative institutions whereas the remaining 14.29% find that the influence ranges between high and very high.
  • All respondents (100%) find that media has moderate influence on executive institutions 100% of polled media organizations believe that media influence to the application of good governance standards by state institutions in Kosovo is at moderate level.

6. Which of the following mechanisms for obtaining information from state institutions do you consider as the most effective?

71.43% of respondents believe that the most effective mechanism for obtaining informations from state are through personal contacts while 28.57% believe on official channels of communications.

7. How would you rate the media access to information provided by the following authorities?

  • 71.43% of respondents rate media access to information provided by judicial institutions to be at a moderate level whereas the remaining 28.57% rate it at a low level.
  • 57.14% of respondents rate media access to information provided by legislative institutions to be at moderate level, 28.57% rate it to be at a low level, and 14.29% at a high level.
  • 57.14% of respondents rate media access to information provided by executive institutions to be at a moderate, 28.57% rate it to be at a high level, and 14.29% at a low level.

8. When asking information from state institutions, how prompt are they in their answers?

  • 71.43% of respondents find that judicial insitutions are moderately prompt in providing answers whereas 28.57% find them to not be prompt.
  • 85.71% of respondents find that legislative insitutions are moderately prompt in providing answers whereas 14.29% find them to not be prompt.
  • All respondents (100%) believe that executive institutions are moderately prompt in providing answers.



CSO and Media

1. To what extent are state institutions in Kosovo transparent and open?

Half of the CSO respondents (50%) believe that executive institutions are moderately open and transparent. Half of the CSO respondents also believe that the legislative institutions are highly open and transparent. 41.6% of CSO respondents believe that judicial institutions are moderately open. 85% of media respondents believe that executive institutions are moderately open and transparent but 57.14% of them believe that legislative and judicial institutions are the one that are moderately open and transparent.

2. To what extent are state institutions in Kosovo accountable for their actions?

Half of the CSO respondents (50%) believe that legislative and executive institutions are moderately accountable. 71.43% of media respondents believe that legislative, judicial, and executive institutions are moderately accountable.

3. To what extent are state institutions in Kosovo efficient in their work?

Most of the polled CSOs and media organizations (66.67% and 71.43% respectively) believe that executive institutions are moderately efficient in their work. 71.43% of CSO respondents believe that legislative institutions are highly efficient and 71.43% of media respondents believe they are moderately efficient. 33.33% of CSOs and 42.86% of media organizations believe that state institutions are not efficient in their work.
